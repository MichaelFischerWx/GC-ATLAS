Lorenz energy cycle · stationary eddies

Reservoirs (J/m², mass-weighted vertical integral):

P_M = (R/2g) ∫ T̂² / (γ p) dp
P_E = (R/2g) ∫ ⟨T*²⟩ / (γ p) dp
K_M = (1/2g) ∫ ([u]² + [v]²) dp
K_E = (1/2g) ∫ ⟨u*² + v*²⟩ dp

where T̂ = [T] − T_ref, T* = T − [T], γ = κT_ref/p − ∂T_ref/∂p, T_ref is set by the toggle above (Lorenz-sorted or area-mean), [·] is zonal mean, and ⟨·⟩ is global area-mean. Leading factor R/(2g) is the canonical Holton–Hakim / Vallis form.

Conversions (W/m²):

C(P_M→P_E) = -(R/g) ∫ ⟨v*T*⟩/(γp) · ∂[T]/∂y dp
              -(R/g) ∫ ⟨ω*T*⟩/(γp) · ∂[T]/∂p dp
C(P_E→K_E) = -(R/g) ∫ ⟨ω*T*⟩ / p dp
C(K_E→K_M) =  (1/g) ∫ ⟨u*v*⟩ cosφ ∂([u]/cosφ)/∂y dp
              + (1/g) ∫ ⟨u*ω*⟩ ∂[u]/∂p dp
C(P_M→K_M) = -(R/g) ∫ [ω][T] / p dp

Stationary eddies only. ' star' here is the deviation from the zonal mean of the monthly-mean field — so this captures the energetics of planetary waves locked to topography / land–sea contrast. The transient-eddy contribution (high-frequency departures from the monthly mean) is excluded; computing it would require daily ERA5, which we don't currently host.

Reference: Lorenz (1955, Tellus); Peixoto & Oort (1992), Physics of Climate, ch. 13–14; Boer (1989, JCli) for the stationary/transient decomposition.

Reference state choice (toggle at top of panel):

  • Lorenz (sorted) — the true minimum-PE reference state from Lorenz (1955): every parcel in the atmosphere is adiabatically re-stratified by θ, with highest θ at the top. Numerically intensive (~780k parcels sorted) but yields the canonical P_M.
  • Area-mean — T_ref(p) = global area-weighted mean of T at each pressure level. The common diagnostic approximation; gives a smaller P_M because it doesn't account for the stratified resorting. Boer (1989) showed this underestimates P_M by ~3×.

Caveats:

  • C(P_M,P_E) here includes both the meridional and vertical eddy-heat-flux terms; many references quote only the dominant meridional term.
  • This is the stationary-eddy cycle — transients (which dominate baroclinic conversion in the literature totals) need daily data, which we don't currently host.

Use the cycle to illustrate structure (which arrows are large, which way energy flows). Toggle the reference state to see how methodology choice changes P_M and the conversions involving the mean state — pedagogically interesting in its own right.

Zonal-mean cross-section

Stationary-eddy angular momentum budget. Per unit mass:

∂[M]/∂t = -(1/(a cosφ))·∂([v][M] cosφ)/∂y - ∂([ω][M])/∂p   mean transport
          -(1/(a cosφ))·∂([v*M*] cosφ)/∂y - ∂([ω*M*])/∂p   stationary eddies
          + F_λ · a cosφ                                    friction + torque

where M = (Ω a cosφ + [u])·a cosφ. [·] = zonal mean, * = stationary eddy departure (M* = a cosφ · u*). In monthly steady-state ∂[M]/∂t ≈ 0, so the implied surface torque (residual) is shown as the negative of the sum.

Display variable: ∂[u]/∂t (m s⁻¹ day⁻¹) = ∂[M]/∂t / (a cosφ) — the implied zonal-wind tendency. Or ∂[M]/∂t scaled to 10⁶ m² s⁻¹ day⁻¹.

Aggregation modes:

  • (lat, p) — full cross-section heatmap (Holton Fig 10.4 style).
  • col-mean — mass-weighted vertical mean (1/p_s)·∫T·dp. Same units as 2D, "what the column-average tendency looks like."
  • ∫dp/g — vertical integral ∫T·(1/g)·dp in N/m² for ∂[u]/∂t form. Directly comparable to surface friction stress (~0.1 N/m²). This is the canonical Peixoto–Oort Fig 11.6 form.

Stationary eddies only. The transient-eddy contribution (sub-monthly variability) is excluded. Per Peixoto–Oort 1992 Table 11.1, stationary [u*v*] is roughly 40% of total in NH winter at 200 hPa (12 vs 30 m²/s²). For SH or annual mean the stationary fraction is smaller. Implied torque magnitudes here will be off vs. published totals by the missing transient amount.

Friction + mountain overlays (active when ews and oro tiles are built): In the all-terms 1D view, two extra lines appear — friction torque τ_f(φ) = -[ews] from observed surface stress, and mountain torque τ_m(φ) = ⟨p_s · ∂h/∂λ⟩/a from surface pressure × orography slope. Their sum is what closes the budget in steady state: (friction + mountain) − (implied torque) ≈ transient-eddy convergence — a direct visual measure of what we're approximating away by using monthly means.

References: Newell et al. (1972, 1974); Oort & Peixoto (1983 atlas); Peixoto & Oort (1992) ch. 11; Holton (textbook) ch. 10; Egger, Weickmann & Hoinka (2007, Rev. Geophys.) for the modern AAM review.
